About me

I am Chris Ostrouchov a graduate student at the University of Tennessee. I am in the Material Science and Engineering department working for Dr. William Weber . My research focus is on the effect of radiation on materials with an emphasis in computation. I have a huge passion for the interesting problems that occur when computer science, mathematics, and material science and engineering can be applied. In the CPSC 462, I hope to learn great habits for managing small and large code (make files, shell/python scripting, testing, and timing), learn more about the UNIX OS (/proc, taskset, etc.), and become familiar with openMPI, openMP, and pthreads.
